Background Knowledge and Preview

My goal for this assignment was to get a good jump start on my final project build (actually putting it together anyway) and to use the RC522 reader in conjunction with the huzzah and to program it first to be able to read UIDs from some NTAG213 NFC tags I had lying around, with the eventual goal of being able to upload this directly into Google Sheets to be used with Appsheet, a protyping platform used often by the company I work for. I started, as I often do, on youtube looking for some good tutorials to follow.

Some good references I found are this as basis for the wiring between the RC522 and the huzzah, but ultimately there was a lot of conflicting information on exactly how to wire specifically where to connect the RST from the RC522 onto the huzzah.

Second, since I already had the stepper motor rails built out and they were one of the few things I actually got working on the first try (pretty fun) I thought about ways I could integrate them into my final project.

One idea that I feel I should explore is simplifying the end goal of my final project to make sure it is acheivable within the remaining time frame. A very useful final product could be a stable, reliable, calibrated testing device for new antenna designs. I could integrate several of the projects already done in class, including the proximity sensor and the stepper motors mainly, to make testing rig that can measure the readable distance of tags reliably. By affixing tags to the rails of the stepper motors and slowly bringing them towards the RC522 and writing a script to print out read distances triggered by succesful reads, I could make a potentially useable product in the field of NFC tag design and fabrication.

Deliverables: RFID

One thing I gathered from all the research was to install Miguel Balboa's RFID Library, the ReadMe and example scripts were honestly the most helpful resources I found anywhere.

This was an extension of my first attempt at wiring up the RC522 succesfully. Although the internal LED would light up indicating that the reader was powering on, I was unable to get any reaction from bringing cards or tags into the field (either success LED flashes or seriel prints)

I knew that I had the correct wiring setup with the configuration aboved so I surmised that one possible reason it wasnt working was a poor connection from simply running wires through the RC522 and trying to cap them. So i went out and borrowed a soldering iron and soldered the connecting cords onto the RC522.
