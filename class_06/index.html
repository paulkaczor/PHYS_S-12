Class 06: 3D Design and Printing

Assignment

1. Design and prepare to print (i.e. slice) either (a) a component you need for your final project or (b) a small object that could not be (easily) made by subtractive methods. Upload your 3D model file (e.g. .f3z), STL file, and sliced gcode file to your documentation.

2. Scan something using a photogrammetry application, if you can get access to one.

Background Knowledge

I started by thinking about useful applications of 3D printing I could implement into my final prject design. Knowing that the eventual goal of designing my final RFID reader would be to have something that it is able to be mounted on the hooks of shipyard cranes a simple housing unit for the reader design seemed a logical place to start.

While looking around Thingiverse and TinkerCAD I came across this design and this design for a snug fit case for an arduino uno and thought if I could make something similar with a slightly larger housing to be able to enclose an rcf522 as well that would be a good start.

Following the tutorial below I made a housing in Fusion360 that would fit our Metro M0 making sure to double check a few dimensions with the micrometer along the way.

I also used the two following tutorials to validate the conecpt of mydesign before purchasing the addiotnal rc522 reader: Interfacing of RFID RC522 with Arduino UNO and https://github.com/miguelbalboa/rfid
